    Welcome to the AI Journey blog!

    Starting a new path of discovery is always exciting. For me, it typically involves a healthy dose of trial and error, experimentation, and learning from mistakes. This blog documents my journey into AI development—the discoveries, challenges, and architectural decisions I'm making as I build progressively better AI systems.

    My goal is to publish semi-regular posts that capture significant changes impacting architecture, where I'll share insights into the reasoning behind each decision. Think of this as a technical journal of an AI journey, from initial exploration to production-ready systems.

    Where I'm Starting From

    I wish I had started documenting this journey about a month ago, when my understanding of AI was roughly on par with the average consumer of Assistive AI—I thought of it as essentially a better search engine. After diving into several books and writing working code, I realize how naive that perspective was.

    After five years of non-stop, high-intensity work on a project that's finally winding down, I have the bandwidth again to focus on learning and exploration. AI has become that focus.

    Here's what I've learned so far:

    • AI is a field, not a single thing—it encompasses machine learning, deep learning, LLMs, agents, and much more
    • Assistive AI is where most people start and what they typically think of when they hear "AI"
    • Agentic AI represents the future—systems that can reason, plan, and act autonomously
    • LLMs are currently the dominant interface layer, but they're just one piece of the puzzle
    • The real power comes from combining LLMs with agentic architectures

    Previous Projects

    I have several earlier projects that I'll document in future posts:

    • My first RAG-powered chatbot
    • Integrating 1.5M questions/answers into a chatbot system
    • Building a base site with mapped RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)

    These projects taught me valuable lessons about data pipelines, retrieval systems, and infrastructure that I'll be building upon.

    What's Next

    For now, I'm getting this blog rolling so I can start tracking daily progress, architectural decisions, and the evolution of my understanding. Stay tuned for technical deep-dives, code examples, and honest reflections on what works and what doesn't.

    Technical Choices

    I'll document key tooling and stack decisions here for high-level tracking. These choices shape the foundation of everything I'm building.

    1. MkDocs for Documentation and Blog

    • High quality and flexible—perfect for technical documentation
    • Large, active community with extensive plugins and themes
    • Markdown-based workflow that integrates well with development processes

    2. GitHub Hosting

    • Cost-effective—using the free tier for now
    • Tightly integrated with public code repositories
    • Minimal learning curve given my existing Git workflow

    3. Python as Primary Language

    • My go-to language with deep prior knowledge
    • The industry standard for data science and AI development
    • Rich ecosystem of libraries (NumPy, PyTorch, LangChain, etc.)
